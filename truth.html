INCIDENT REPORT - MARCH 1967

Filed by: Director Sarah Whitmore | Date: March 21, 1967

During the annual school trip to Pinewood Valley (March 15-21, 1967), a group of students discovered the remains of an old research facility in the restricted eastern sector of the valley.

Against explicit instructions, seven students entered the facility on the evening of March 18th. They were missing for approximately 14 hours.

When the students were found, they exhibited signs of severe psychological distress and reported experiencing shared hallucinations involving temporal anomalies and the presence of something they described as "watching from outside time".

Most concerning was their insistence that they had been in the facility for much longer than 14 hours - they claimed weeks had passed, though all watches and recording devices confirmed the actual timeline.

FOLLOW-UP REPORT - APRIL 1967

Filed by: Dr. Michael Chen, Psychiatric Consultant

All seven students continue to report identical "memories" of their time in the facility. They describe finding journals dating back to 1952, written in increasingly erratic handwriting, documenting experiments with something the writers called "the threshold".

The students claim that the facility was built by the original founders of Pinewood Valley Lodge, who were not educators but researchers investigating reports of temporal anomalies in the valley dating back centuries.

Most disturbing is their shared conviction that they are still in the facility, and everything since March 18th has been what they call "the reflection" - a loop that prevents those who find the truth from ever truly leaving.

ADMINISTRATIVE DECISION - MAY 1967

Pinewood Valley Board of Directors

After extensive consultation, the Board has decided to:

  • Permanently seal the eastern sector facility
  • Remove all references to pre-1952 valley history from official materials
  • Continue operating the educational retreat program with enhanced safety protocols
  • Monitor the affected students (names redacted for privacy)
  • Maintain public story that the facility never existed

The valley remains safe for educational purposes when proper boundaries are respected. The incident was isolated and all affected students have been released from psychiatric care, though three continue to insist they never left the facility.

NOTE TO FUTURE STAFF: If students report any of the following, contact emergency protocols immediately:

  • Finding structures or buildings not on current maps
  • Time discrepancies or "missing hours"
  • Feelings of being "watched" by something non-physical
  • Claims of finding documents dated before 1952
  • Insistence that "the valley remembers"
