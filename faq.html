Frequently Asked Questions

Cell service is limited in the valley, but the lodge has Wi-Fi you can access during free time. We encourage students to disconnect and enjoy nature, but we understand you'll want to check in with family!

Yes, absolutely. All medications must be turned in to the trip nurse upon arrival with a completed medication consent form signed by your parent/guardian. You'll receive your medication from the nurse at the appropriate times.

We have 42 students signed up for this trip, plus 6 chaperones. Perfect size for a close-knit adventure!

You'll be staying in cabins with 6-8 students per cabin. Each cabin has bunk beds, heating, and its own bathroom. Cabins are divided by gender, and each has an assigned chaperone nearby.

Safety is our top priority! We have certified wilderness first aid instructors and a nurse on site. Minor scrapes and bruises happen, but we've never had a serious incident. Well, not since 1967.

It's totally normal! Our chaperones are experienced with homesickness and will help you through it. You can also call home during designated phone times. Most students find that staying busy with activities helps a lot!

For your safety, please stay within marked trails and designated areas. The old cabin ruins beyond the east trail are off-limits - the structures are unstable. The lodge management asks that you do not enter this area under any circumstances.

Pinewood Valley Lodge was established in 1952 as an educational retreat. Thousands of students have enjoyed the beauty of this location over the decades! The valley itself is much older, of course - the indigenous peoples considered it sacred ground. Some said it was a place where the veil between worlds grows thin.
